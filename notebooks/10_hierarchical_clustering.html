Stage 01

When to Use Hierarchical Clustering

Hierarchical clustering builds a dendrogram — a tree that shows how data points merge into groups. The key advantage: you decide how many clusters after seeing the structure, not before.

  • Identify the use case Hierarchical is best when you need a taxonomy (product categories, incident types) or when you cannot pre-specify K.Limitation: computationally O(n2). Use only on datasets <50K rows. For large datasets, use K-Means.
  • Choose Ward linkage as default Ward minimises within-cluster variance when merging — produces compact, well-separated clusters. Other linkage options (complete, average, single) exist but Ward is the reliable default.
  • Scale features Same as K-Means — Euclidean distance-based, sensitive to scale.

Stage 02

Build Dendrogram & Choose K

Fit the hierarchical model and read the dendrogram to determine the natural number of clusters.

  • Fit AgglomerativeClustering(linkage='ward') The algorithm merges closest pairs iteratively. Record the merge distances (heights) at each step.
  • Plot the dendrogram Y-axis = merge distance (height). X-axis = individual observations. A large gap between two consecutive merge heights suggests a natural K. Cut at the height where the gap is largest.
  • Translate dendrogram cut to cluster assignments Cutting the dendrogram at height=6.5 produces 5 clusters. Each observation is assigned to its cluster at that cut.

Stage 03

QA

  • Verify Ward linkage is appropriate Ward is best for compact, roughly equal-sized clusters. Complete linkage for outlier-resistant clustering. Single linkage for elongated 'chaining' structures.
  • Check dataset size Hierarchical clustering memory is O(n2). At 50K rows: 2.5 billion distance pairs. This will likely crash or take hours.
Output: QA Checklist
Good
  • Ward linkage used (or other linkage justified)
  • Dendrogram visualised before choosing K
  • Dataset size < 50K rows
  • Cluster sizes reasonable (no cluster < 1% of total)
Red flags
  • Dataset > 50K rows (will be slow/infeasible)
  • K chosen without looking at dendrogram
  • Single linkage used (produces 'chaining' — all points cluster together progressively)
  • Clusters validated only by silhouette — no business naming test
