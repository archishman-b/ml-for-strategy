Stage 01

Why Percentiles Matter More Than Means

Standard regression predicts the mean. For planning decisions, you need a specific percentile. Quantile regression predicts exactly that.

  • Identify the right quantile Q50: central estimate. Q75-Q85: moderate risk buffer. Q90-Q95: high-reliability planning. Q99: regulatory tail risk.Ask: what happens when actual demand exceeds prediction? If severe, use Q90+.
  • Understand the asymmetric loss At Q90, under-prediction is penalised 9x more than over-prediction. The model learns to output a value exceeded only 10% of the time.
  • Fit one model per quantile Separate model for Q50, Q75, Q90 — each optimised for its quantile's asymmetric loss.

Stage 02

Train Multiple Quantile Models

Fit QuantileRegressor at each target quantile and compare predictions.

  • Fit Q50, Q75, Q90 models sklearn QuantileRegressor minimises quantile loss. Same feature set, different loss function.
  • Check for quantile crossing Q90 predictions must always exceed Q50. If they cross, apply isotonic regression post-processing.
  • Compare against mean + fixed buffer Quantile regression must outperform naive buffer — otherwise no justification for added complexity.

Stage 03

QA Checklist

Validate before deploying quantile predictions for business planning.

    Output: QA Checklist
    Good output
    • Coverage rate empirically validated (~10% exceedances for Q90)
    • No quantile crossing (Q90 > Q50 for all observations)
    • Compared against mean + buffer baseline
    • Quantile chosen based on explicit cost-of-stockout discussion
    Red flags
    • Q90 treated as a guarantee (it will be exceeded ~10% of the time — that is correct, not a failure)
