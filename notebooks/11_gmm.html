Gaussian Mixture Models

Question: Who partially belongs to multiple segments?Output: Soft membership probabilitiesDataset: customers.csv (800 rows)Use case: Customer genome soft segmentation · Transition-zone intervention targeting

Pipeline walkthrough — analyst workflow steps, pre-populated outputs, and business interpretation at each stage.

Stage 01

Soft Segmentation Setup

GMM assigns each customer a probability of belonging to each segment rather than a hard label. This reflects real customer complexity — a customer can be 70% Champion, 30% At-Risk.

  • Identify whether soft segments are needed Hard segments (K-Means) are sufficient for most strategy work. Use GMM when: (1) you need membership probabilities for personalisation, or (2) you suspect segments genuinely overlap.If you need 'Customer X belongs to segment 2', use K-Means. If you need 'Customer X is 68% segment 2', use GMM.
  • Choose covariance type Full: most flexible, each cluster has its own covariance matrix. Tied: all clusters share one covariance. Diagonal: features independent within clusters. Spherical: circular, like K-Means. Start with 'full'; use 'diagonal' if n/cluster is small.
  • Use BIC for model selection BIC (Bayesian Information Criterion) penalises model complexity. Compute BIC for K=2 to 10. Choose K at the BIC minimum or elbow.

Stage 02

Fit & Interpret Soft Assignments

Fit the GMM and examine membership probabilities — the key output that differentiates GMM from hard clustering.

  • Fit GaussianMixture(n_components=5, covariance_type='full') Fitted via EM algorithm. Each iteration assigns soft membership weights then recomputes component parameters.
  • Extract membership probabilities For each customer: vector of 5 probabilities summing to 1. High certainty: [0.93, 0.02, 0.02, 0.02, 0.01]. Low certainty: [0.31, 0.28, 0.22, 0.11, 0.08] — ambiguous customer.
  • Identify ambiguous customers Customers with entropy > threshold are genuinely segment-ambiguous. These are interesting: they may be transitional customers who are in the process of churning or upgrading.

What this tells you
  • 68% of customers have confident segment assignments (entropy < 0.5) — the segmentation is well-defined for most of the population.
  • The 14% ambiguous customers are disproportionately valuable: higher churn rate (31%) and mid-tenure (24 months) suggests they are at a tipping point. GMM identifies them; K-Means would have assigned them arbitrarily to one segment.
  • Customer C0551 (33/31/22/08/06 probabilities) is a genuine strategic question: is this customer churning from Champion to At-Risk, or At-Risk to Loyal?
GMM's unique value: identifying segment transitionsThe most strategically valuable output of GMM is the 14% ambiguous customers — K-Means would have assigned them to a single segment, hiding the ambiguity. These transition-zone customers are where proactive intervention has the highest ROI.
Stage 03

QA

  • Run multiple initialisations GMM can converge to local optima. Run n_init=5-10 and check if component parameters are consistent.
  • Check BIC plateau If BIC keeps decreasing as K increases, consider a higher max K or a different covariance type.
Output: QA Checklist
Good
  • BIC used for K selection (not just silhouette)
  • Multiple initialisations run
  • Ambiguous customers identified and interpreted
  • Membership probabilities shown alongside hard assignments
Red flags
  • K chosen without BIC — arbitrary
  • Single initialisation — may be local optimum
  • Hard labels only reported — soft probabilities discarded
  • n_init=1 (default in some libraries — check)
