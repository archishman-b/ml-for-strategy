Notebook 2.1

Ridge / Lasso Regression

Question: What drives this KPI?Output: Continuous valueDataset: pricing.csv (600 rows)Use case: Margin driver analysis · CLTV modelling

Pipeline walkthrough — analyst workflow steps, followed by pre-populated outputs and business interpretation at each stage.

Stage 01

Driver Analysis Setup

Ridge and Lasso identify what drives a continuous KPI. Key question: explain all drivers (Ridge) or find the minimal set (Lasso)?

  • Define continuous target pricing.csv target = gross_margin %. Features: product category, list_price, discount_pct, volume, channel, competitor_price_index, cost_index.
  • Standardise all features first Mandatory. After scaling, coefficient magnitude is directly comparable across all features.Without standardisation a coefficient of 0.02 on revenue and 2.0 on a binary flag are not comparable.
  • Choose Ridge vs Lasso Ridge: all features contribute, handles multicollinearity. Lasso: zeros out irrelevant features. For driver analysis: Ridge. For feature selection: Lasso.

Stage 02

Train & Interpret Coefficients

Fit Ridge with alpha selected via cross-validation. Extract and interpret standardised coefficients.

  • Try alpha = 0.01, 0.1, 1, 10, 100 on validation RMSE CV selects alpha=1.0 as optimal.
  • Plot coefficient chart Horizontal bar chart sorted by magnitude. Positive = increases margin, negative = decreases.
  • Sanity-check every coefficient direction Higher discounts should reduce margin. Lower cost should increase margin. Any counter-intuitive result needs investigation.

What this tells you
  • Discount policy is the primary margin lever — 1σ reduction in discount (~4pp improvement) predicts +5pp margin increase.
  • Direct channel delivers +2.4pp vs distributor — channel mix shift is a meaningful opportunity.
  • R2=0.71 means 29% of margin variance is unexplained — likely by product-specific factors not in the current feature set.
Causation vs correlationCoefficients describe association. Higher volume may appear to drive margin due to self-selection (profitable products sell more). Design a test to confirm causal direction before acting on any finding.
Stage 03

Residual Check & QA

Check model assumptions and sign off on the output.

  • Plot residuals vs fitted values Constant variance across the range = heteroscedasticity absent. Funnel shape = needs log transform or weighted regression.
  • Compare Ridge vs OLS Ridge should always match or outperform OLS on hold-out data when features are correlated.
Output: Residual Diagnostics Console
Residuals vs fitted: roughly constant variance across range ✓ Shapiro-Wilk test: p=0.12 (approximately normal residuals) ✓ Outliers (|std residual|>3): 4 observations (0.7%) — review for data errors Conclusion: model assumptions broadly satisfied. Safe to interpret.
Output: QA Checklist
Good output
  • Features standardised before coefficient extraction
  • Ridge used over plain OLS
  • R2 and RMSE compared against intercept-only baseline
  • Causation vs correlation explicit in findings
Red flags
  • Coefficients reported in raw unstandardised units
  • Top feature is high-cardinality ID or categorical
  • RMSE without context vs baseline
