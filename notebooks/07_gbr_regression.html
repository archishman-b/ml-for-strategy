Stage 01

Nonlinear Regression Setup

GBR is the right tool when the relationship between features and target is genuinely nonlinear. Always establish a Ridge baseline first.

  • Establish Ridge baseline Fit Ridge first. If R2 >= 0.70 and RMSE acceptable, Ridge is sufficient.GBR is justified only when it improves R2 by >0.03 AND the use case does not require simple coefficient interpretation.
  • Choose loss function based on error cost MSE: minimises average squared error. MAE: robust to outliers. Huber: compromise. Quantile: for percentile estimates. Use Huber for claims/costs with rare extreme values.
  • Choose library LightGBM: fastest for large datasets. XGBoost: most tuneable. sklearn GBR: simplest but slowest.
Output: Baseline Comparison Console

Stage 02

Train & Feature Importance

Train with early stopping, extract permutation importance, and build partial dependence plots for top features.

  • learning_rate=0.05, early stopping Large n_estimators=1000 with early_stopping_rounds=50. Model stops at optimal point automatically.
  • Permutation importance over MDI Permutation importance: how much does RMSE increase when this feature is shuffled? Unbiased across feature types.
  • Partial dependence plots Fix all other features at median. Vary one feature from min to max. Plot predicted margin at each value. Reveals the nonlinear shape.

Output: Partial Dependence — Discount vs Margin Chart
Partial dependence plot shows: 0% discount → ~44% predicted margin 5% discount → ~42% predicted margin (−2pp) 10% discount → ~35% predicted margin (−9pp from 0%) 15% discount → ~26% predicted margin (−18pp from 0%) 20% discount → ~21% predicted margin (−23pp from 0%) Key nonlinear finding: Marginal cost of each additional discount point increases sharply above 10%. First 5pp of discount: loses 2pp margin. Second 5pp (5→10%): loses 7pp margin. Third 5pp (10→15%): loses 9pp more margin. This inflection at 10% is the key business insight — discounts above 10% are disproportionately margin-destructive. An optimal discount cap is ~9-10%.
What this tells you
  • The partial dependence plot reveals a nonlinear relationship — first 5% of discount loses 2pp margin, but discounts above 10% become disproportionately margin-destructive.
  • This inflection point is invisible to Ridge (which assumes constant marginal effect per unit of discount). GBR captures the actual shape.
  • The 10% threshold becomes an actionable pricing policy recommendation: set a hard discount cap at 9-10%.
GBR cannot extrapolate beyond training rangeIf training discounts ranged 0-20%, predictions at 25% discount are unreliable. The model anchors to the training range. Always check production scoring feature distributions against training boundaries.
